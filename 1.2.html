Chapter 1.2 – Types of Money

My Notes

Commodity Money

Money that has value itself, as a commodity, and can be used for exchange.

Examples: Gold coins, silver coins, shells.

Characteristic: Intrinsic value equal to commodity value.

Representative Money

Certificates or tokens that can be exchanged for a fixed amount of a commodity.

Examples: Gold or silver certificates.

Characteristic: Represents real commodity (e.g., gold).

Fiat Money

Money by government decree that has no intrinsic value but is legal tender.

Examples: Indian Rupee, US Dollar, Euro.

Characteristic: Backed by government trust, not gold.

Credit or Commercial Bank Money

Money created by banks in the form of deposits.

Examples: Bank account balances, checks, UPI.

Characteristic: Exists digitally, not in cash.

Fiduciary Money

Money based on trust in its issuer, not legal tender.

Examples: Bank drafts, promissory notes.

Characteristic: Relies on issuer's promise.

Digital Money

Money that exists only in electronic or digital form.

Examples: Bitcoin, Ethereum, CBDCs.

Characteristic: Blockchain-based, no physical form.
