Understanding Income and Expenses

Income and Expenses

Income is the total money you receive from sources like pocket money, part-time jobs, or allowances. Expenses are divided into fixed and variable.

By tracking your expenses, you can identify spending patterns, reduce unnecessary costs, and make smarter financial choices.

Types of Expenses: Fixed expenses (predictable, e.g., rent, subscriptions) and Variable expenses (flexible, e.g., entertainment, shopping).

The 50/30/20 Rule

An effective tool for balancing spending and saving is the 50/30/20 rule:

Category Percentage Description
Needs 50% Essentials like rent, groceries, transportation, and utility bills.
Wants 30% Personal treats such as entertainment, dining out, or shopping.
Savings/Debt 20% Money set aside for future goals, emergencies, or paying off loans.
