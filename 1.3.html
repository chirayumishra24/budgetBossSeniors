What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is digital money secured by cryptography and powered by blockchain technology. It is decentralized, meaning no central authority controls it. Transactions are secure, transparent, and conducted peer-to-peer without intermediaries. Cryptocurrencies are used for payments, investments, and innovations like smart contracts and DeFi (Decentralized Finance).

Bitcoin: The Pioneer Cryptocurrency

Bitcoin is the first and most famous cryptocurrency, created in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto.

It runs on a peer-to-peer network using a blockchain — a public digital ledger that records all transactions without banks or governments.

Key Aspects:

  • Mining: Computers verify transactions through cryptography and add them to the blockchain.
  • Limited Supply: Only 21 million bitcoins will ever exist.
  • Features: Decentralized, secure, transparent, and global.
  • Challenges: Price volatility and high energy use.
Characteristic: Can be decentralized (cryptocurrencies) or centralized (CBDCs), used for online transactions.

Currency Systems and Fun Fact

Currency Systems: Governments issue their own currencies and regulate their circulation; different countries have different currency systems.

Fun Fact: Currency is the official money issued by a government, such as Indian Rupees, US Dollars, Euros, etc. It comes in coins and paper notes with different values.
