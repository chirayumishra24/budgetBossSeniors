Chapter 2.2: Basics of Saving

My Notes

What is Saving?

Saving means setting aside a part of your income regularly for future needs or emergencies. It is one of the most important habits for building financial security and achieving long-term goals.

Key Insight: Saving helps you prepare for emergencies, achieve goals, and build financial security.

Why Save?

Benefits of Saving

Savings help you reach both short-term and long-term goals. They act as a safety net during emergencies and allow your money to grow through interest.

  • Emergency Fund: Protects you from financial stress.
  • Growth through Interest: Money grows faster with compounding.
Tip: The earlier you start saving, the faster your money grows through compounding interest.

Where and How to Save

Saving Options and Mechanisms

Choose the right place to save based on your goals and safety needs.

  • Piggy Bank: For small, short-term savings.
  • Bank Account: Safer and earns interest.
Pro Tip: Automate savings with regular transfers to stay consistent.

How Much to Save

Savings Goals

Set clear savings targets to balance immediate needs and future goals.

  • Emergency Fund: 3–6 months of expenses.
  • Regular Savings: Save 10% of income.
Key Habit: Treat saving as a priority, not leftover spending.
